Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Banaras, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and the spiritual heart of India. Situated on the banks of the sacred River Ganges, it is a city of temples, rituals, and mysticism.

The Ghats of Varanasi are iconic, with thousands of devotees and tourists gathering every day to witness the mesmerizing Ganga Aarti. The Assi, Dashashwamedh, and Manikarnika Ghats hold significant cultural and religious importance, serving as a gateway to salvation for Hindus.

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and a major pilgrimage destination. The temple's golden spire and spiritual energy attract millions of devotees seeking divine blessings.

Beyond religious sites, Varanasi is home to Sarnath, where Lord Buddha gave his first sermon after attaining enlightenment. The Dhamek Stupa, the Ashoka Pillar, and the Sarnath Museum preserve the rich history of Buddhism.
