The Mysore Palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is one of India's most stunning architectural wonders and the pride of Karnataka. Nestled in the heart of Mysore, this grand palace was once the royal residence of the Wadiyar dynasty.

Constructed in the Indo-Saracenic style, the palace blends Hindu, Muslim, Rajput, and Gothic architectural elements. The palace is adorned with intricately carved doors, beautiful stained glass windows, and an opulent durbar hall.

One of the most mesmerizing sights of the palace is during the Mysore Dasara festival when it is illuminated with nearly 100,000 light bulbs, transforming it into a dazzling spectacle.

The palace grounds also house several temples, including the Someshvara Temple and Lakshmi Ramana Temple, showcasing the rich spiritual and cultural heritage of Mysore.

Every year, millions of tourists visit the Mysore Palace to witness its grandeur, explore its history, and experience its regal charm.
