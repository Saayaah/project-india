Kerala Backwaters - The Venice of the East

The Kerala Backwaters, often referred to as the “Venice of the East,” are a network of serene lagoons, lakes, and canals stretching along the coast of Kerala. This picturesque region is one of India’s most enchanting travel destinations.

One of the best ways to explore the Kerala Backwaters is by houseboat. These luxurious floating homes offer a unique way to experience the lush green landscapes, traditional villages, and abundant wildlife. Alleppey, often called the “Venice of India,” is the most famous starting point for a backwater cruise.

Kumarakom, a beautiful village on the banks of Vembanad Lake, is another must-visit spot. This region is home to the famous Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, where travelers can witness a variety of migratory birds in their natural habitat.

The Kuttanad region, often called the “Rice Bowl of Kerala,” is one of the few places in the world where farming is done below sea level. This scenic landscape, with its endless paddy fields and interwoven waterways, offers a glimpse into Kerala’s agrarian lifestyle.

For those looking for a more immersive experience, staying in a homestay along the backwaters allows visitors to witness traditional Kerala life up close. The local cuisine, featuring fresh seafood, coconut-based curries, and delicious appams, is a highlight of any trip to the backwaters.

The tranquil ambiance, slow-paced lifestyle, and breathtaking views make the Kerala Backwaters a perfect destination for relaxation and rejuvenation.
